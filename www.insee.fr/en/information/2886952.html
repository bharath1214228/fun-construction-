            October 2022

            14/10/2022 - The annual change of the Housing Rent Reference Index (RRI) is capped

            In accordance with Article 12 of the 2022-1158 Act on Emergency Measures for the Protection of Purchasing Power of 16 August 2022, the annual change of the housing rent reference index may not exceed 3.5% between the third quarter of 2022 and the second quarter of 2023.
            By way of derogation, it may not exceed 2.5% in the collectivities governed by article 73 of the Constitution (overseas regions and departments) and, in accordance with prefectorial order R20-2022-10-11-00012, it may not exceed 2.0% in Corsica between the third quarter of 2022 and the second quarter of 2023.
            From the third quarter 2022, Insee publishes four new series : On one side, the housing rent reference index in the collectivities governed by article 73 of the Constitution (010760509)and the related annual change (010760510), and on the other side, the housing rent reference index in Corsica (010760507) and the related annual change (010760508). The values of two other series (001515333 and 001515334) are still applicable on the rest of the national territory.

            September 2022

            15/09/2022 - Building, civil engineering and various indices for construction

            Revision of BT18a and BT19b index headings :
            From the publication of index for July 2022, onwards, the word “wood” is added to the headings of BT18a and BT19b indices in order to clarify these headings. The heading of the BT18a index now reads as follow: “Inside wood joinery” and that of the BT19b index now reads as follow: “Outside wood joinery”. These changes neither affect the composition nor the calculation of these indices. It is only intended to better reflect their actual composition.

            Update of the composition of the BT16b, BT18a, BT19b and BT54 indices :
            From the publication of index for July 2022, onwards, the weights and inputs of BT16b “Wood frame”, BT18a “Inside wood joinery”, BT19b “Outside wood joinery” and BT54 “Wood framework” indices are updated in order to better take into account the costs incurred in these activities. The new weights and inputs are detailed in the notice published in the Official Journal referring to the release of the July 2022 indices and in the methodological note « Composition détaillée des index BT en base 2010 ». In July 2022, the BT16b, BT18a, BT19b and BT54 indices monthly evolutions are calculated from their new compositions and are chaine-linked to their June 2022 value in order to obtain the July 2022 value, without revising previously disseminated values.

            Update of the composition of TP08 and TP09 indices :
            From the publication of index for July 2022, onwards, the weights and inputs of the "Energy" item of TP08 “Roads development works and maintenance” and TP09 “Making and implementation of mixes” indices are updated in order to better take into account the costs incurred in these activities. The new weights and inputs are detailed in the notice published in the Official Journal referring to the release of the indices for July 2022 and in the methodological note « Composition détaillée des index TP en base 2010 ». In July 2022, the TP08 and TP09 indices monthly evolutions are calculated based on their new weights and inputs and are chain-linked to their June 2022 value in order to obtain the July 2022 value, without revising the previously disseminated values.

            July 2022

            29/07/2022 - Operations on goods and services: adding two series

            To contain the rise in fuel prices, a 15 cent per litre tax-free rebate is applied since April 1st 2022. In national accounts, this rebate is accounted as a subsidy on products (D319) on coke and refined petroleum products (A17-C2 in the QNA nomenclature).
            As this type of subsidy is new for this product, two new time series (in value and volume) has been created to take account of the new subsidy. In value, the series is equal to the estimate of the amount of the tax-free subsidy. In volumes chained at previous year prices, the estimates is made by applying to the tax base the tax rate in force during base year, 2014, which 0 in that case, as the subsidy didn’t exists at the time : the series is thus 0 for the whole period. Those amounts are taken into accounts in all aggregates and accounting equation.

            09/06/2022 -  Methodological changes of the Quarterly Employment Estimates

            02/06/2022 -  Rebasing of the year-end employment series

            For the release of the final 2019 Employment Estimates, the year-end employment series are released in 2018 base. This means that end-2018 levels are recalibrated to those measured in the statistical source from employer and self-employed returns. The previous estimates were released in 2007 base. They had been extended between 2008 and 2018 by chaining evolution rates. Over the years, a slight discrepancy with the level of the detailed source may have occurred. The entire series has therefore been revised for the entire period. The methodology of this rebasing and the associated revisions, in level and in evolution, are detailed in the Insee Results devoted to the Annual Employment Estimates.
            In addition, new series are now available here. The end-of-year employment series calculated in base 2007 are discontinued, but remain accessible and are linked to the new series in base 2018.

            04/03/2022 – Methodological changes in the industrial production index

            Since the move to the new basis 2015 in March 2018, the industrial production indices (IPI) are reviewed at the rate of one fifth of the series each year. The aim is to improve the relevance of the IPI with regards to economic and technological evolutions, especially by including emerging products or removing declining products. The fourth annual review is implemented with this publication in March 2022. The coverage of the industrial production index is improved for the manufacture of wearing apparel and for the manufacture of other electrical equipment. A new series is now available: the manufacture of sports goods. The adjusted series have been backcasted from 2019, which can generate revisions on these indices up to that date.
            In parallel with this modification, seasonal and working-day adjustment (SA-WDA) models have been reestimated on the recent past, in order to improve the quality of the index, and weights have been updated.

            11/02/2022 - Collective tourist accommodation occupancy survey : revision of the method of missing data imputation

            As of January 2020 for outdoor accomodations and for Other Short-Stay Accomodations (OSSA), missing data for non-sampled or non-responding accomodations are imputed according to a new method, using econometric models based on the characteristics of the accomodations. This new method of non-response imputation leads to slightly reduce the total number of overnight stays, but has no impact on quarterly trends. In order to be able to calculate any evolution with newly calculated data, Insee has recalculated 2019 data. These backcast series are used in the Information Rapid publications from the 2019 first quarter. A correspondence table between old and new series is available at the Correspondence tables web page.

            10/03/2020 - Reviewing of the methodology of the the industrial production indices

            Since the swap to the new basis 2015 in march 2018, the industrial production indices (IPI) are reviewed at the rate of one fifth of the series each year. This annual update will make it possible to improve the relevance of the IPI with regards to the evolution of technologies and production processes, by adapting the way of measuring such product, by including emerging products or, on the contrary, removing old products. The second annual review is implemented with this publication in march 2020. It has improved slightly the coverage of the industrial production index and has lead to introduce two new indices: manufacture of other products of wood; manufacture of articles of cork, straw and plaiting materials and manufacture of jewellery and related articles. The adjusted series have been backward-reviewed from 2017. This can generate revisions on indices, even if they are rather moderate at an aggregated level. In parallel with this modification, Seasonnal and working-day ajustment (SA-WDA models) are reestimated on a sub-period (since 2005) to adjust indices on the recent past, in order to provide improved fit and better robustness.

            February 2020

            07/02/2020 - The use of scanner data for calculating the Consumer Price Index

            From January 2020 on, the French CPI uses scanner data on the scope of manufactured food products, of cleaning products and of the products for personal care sold in supermarket and hypermarket in metropolitan France.
            The huge number of data improves the quality of the index. In 2019, an impact study was carried out. This impact on the all-item index is relatively low (-0.08 points at most on the index; 0.1 points in certain months on the annual or monthly change). However, scanner data impacts are higher on classes for which the use of scanner data is more important.
            Scanner data makes it possible to follow two additional Coicop sub-classes (02.1.3.3 beers with low alcohol content or without alcohol and 02.1.3.4 beer-based beverage). Contrariwise, some average prices will no longer be published, because of conceptual differences in the computing of average prices with the scanner data (use of the daily purchased quantities and better tracking of special offers).

            Since 2018Q2, the measurement of the job seekers’ level of training has been disturbed by the implementation by Pôle Emploi of the online service "skills profile" for job seekers.
            Without affecting the number of people registered at Pôle emploi, this change however modified their distribution by level of training. It would thus have led to a 4 point downward revision of the part of job seekers with a training level below the baccalauréat (from 56% to 52% after the setting of the “skill profile” service) registered in categories A, B and C.
            From now on, the series by level of training are published according to this new measure, and begin in 2018Q1 instead of 1996Q1 for the series published until then. The “historical” series, according to the methodology prior to the implementation of the “skill profile” service, remain available (over the period from 1996Q1 to 2018Q1).

            March 2019

            08/03/2019 - Reviewing of the methodology of the industrial production indices

            Since the implementation of the new basis 2015 in March 2018, the industrial production indices (IPI) are reviewed at the rate of one fifth of the series each year. This annual update will make it possible to improve the relevance of the IPI with regards to the evolution of technologies and production processes, by adapting the way of measuring a product, by including emerging products or, on the contrary, removing old products. The first annual review is implemented with this publication in march 2019. It has significantly improved the coverage of the industrial production index and has lead to introduce three new indices : Industrial manufacture of bread and fresh pastry, Manufacture of other articles of paper and paperboard and Pre-press and pre-media services. The adjusted series have been backward-reviewed from 2015. This can generate revisions on indices, even if they are rather moderate at an aggregated level. In parallel with this modification, SA-WDA models have been reestimated on a sub-period (since 2005) to adjust indices on the recent past, in order to provide improved fit and better robustness (SA-WDA models were until now estimated on the whole period, i.e. 1990 to 2019).

            22/01/2019 - Changes in the serie on contribution rate and guaranteed minimum wages

            From the merger of schemes Agirc and Arrco on 1st January 2019, employer and employee contributions for the supplementary pension schemes have disappeared and have been replaced by contributions Agirc-Arrco, according to the wage bracket. Bracket 1 applies on the portion of the wage that is less than 1 cap, and bracket 2 on the portion of the wage that ranges between 1 and 8 caps. The AGFF contribution also disappeared and two new contributions appeared : the overall contribution balance (CEG) and the technical contribution balance (CET).
