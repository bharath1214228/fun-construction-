            23/2/2022 - Redesign of enterprise births calculation

            Starting with the release of the Informations Rapides outlining the results for January 2022, enterprise births have been calculated according to a revised scheme. This new method of calculation broadens the scope of companies taken into account, with the aim of better covering the scope of business statistics. This results in particular in an increase in the level of business creation.
            All the series have been recalculated on this new scope since January 2012. Backcasting of the national sectorial series has been carried out since 2000. The series of enterprises births calculated according to the previous method have been stopped, but remain available. A correspondence table between old and new series is available below.
            The correspondance table has been enriched the April 15th, 2022.

            11/02/2022 - Revision of the series concerning hotels from January 1st, 2019
            Collective tourist accommodation occupancy survey

            As of January 2020 for outdoor accomodations and for Other Short-Stay Accomodations (OSSA), missing data for non-sampled or non-responding accomodations are imputed according to a new method, using econometric models based on the characteristics of the accomodations. This new method of non-response imputation leads to slightly reduce the total number of overnight stays, but has no impact on quarterly trends. In order to be able to calculate any evolution with newly calculated data, Insee has recalculated 2019 data. These backcast series are used in the Information Rapid publications from the 2019 first quarter.

            Correspondence table
            (xlsx, 23 Ko)

            11/07/2019 - Revision of the series concerning hotels from January 1st, 2019
            Collective tourist accommodation occupancy survey

            As of January 2019, missing data for non-sampled or non-responding hotels are imputed according to a new method, using econometric models based on the characteristics of the hotels. The characteristics are defined according to the most important criteria explanations of tourist attendance (previous period's response, ranking, size, type of management, geographical area).
            To compare the 2019 data with previous years, INSEE retropolated the data on hotels over the period 2011 to 2018. These backcast series are used in the Information Rapid publications from the first quarter of 2019.
            This new method of imputing non-response tends to slightly reduce the total number of overnight stays, but has no impact on quarterly trends.
            Revision of the serie concerning hotels: the correspondance table has been enriched the April 24th, 2020.

            Correspondence table
            (xls, 107 Ko)

            06/09/2018 - New methodology for the calculation of the Notaries-INSEE second-hand dwellings

            The Notaries-INSEE second-hand dwellings prices indices are now published in a new version. Their elaboration is based on a revised and improved method of calculation. The scope has been extended to the overseas departments and is now France (excluding Mayotte). This new method is applied from the revised publication of Q1 2018. The reference is from now on the average in 2015 (against T1 2010 until the publication of T1 2018).
            All the data based on T1 2010 have been stopped and replaced by new data based on average 2015. You should find below a correspondence table between old and new series that contains “Idbank” identifiers and titles in base 2010 and “Idbank” identifiers and titles in base 2015.

            Correspondence table

            26/02/2016 - Producer price indices of French industry: new series for Electricity

            Since January 1st 2016, the regulated electricity selling prices have been removed for the consumers subscribing high power (more than 36 kVA). This measure is linked to the legislative frameworks adopted with the opening of the electricity market to competition since 2000.

            These evolutions lead INSEE to create new Electricity producer price indices for the french market and to revamp their dissemination.

            Concerning Purchaser's prices for contract escalation in the Producer price indices of French industry for the French market, the identifiers of the series stopped in December 2015, those of the next series and the correspondence table are available here:
